Metric Explanations:

What it measures: Average percentage error, symmetric for over and under-forecasting.

Interpretation: Lower values are better. Values below 20% indicate good accuracy.

Formula: 100% × mean(|actual - forecast| / ((|actual| + |forecast|)/2))

What it measures: Forecast accuracy relative to a simple naive forecast.

Interpretation: Values < 1.0 mean your model beats naive forecasting. Lower is better.

Benchmark: 1.0 = same as naive, <0.5 = excellent, >2.0 = poor

What it measures: Quality of the entire forecast distribution, not just point estimates.

Interpretation: Lower values indicate better probabilistic forecasts. Considers uncertainty.

Advantage: Rewards well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
